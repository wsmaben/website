COVID-19 Patient Education

Patient Handouts/Downloads on COVID-19

The Washington State Department of Health offers three one-pagers for people who have or think they may have COVID-19 that physicians and providers can distribute to their patients or post on their organization's website.

  • What to do if you have confirmed or suspected COVID-19 (March 17, 2020)
  • What to do if you were potentially exposed to someone with COVID-19 (March 17, 2020)
  • What to do if you have symptoms of COVID-19 and have not been around anyone who has been diagnosed with COVID-19 (March 17, 2020)

The DOH posted a helpful FAQ: COVID-19 Testing Process: What You Need to Know. (March 9, 2020)

Multilingual COVID-19 patient resources

For patients who don’t speak English, state and public agencies offer COVID-19 educational resources in multiple languages.

  • Washington State Department of Health Multilingual COVID-19 Educational Materials
  • Public Health - Seattle & King County Multilingual Novel Coronavirus Fact Sheets
  • Office of Immigrant and Refugee Affairs (OIRA) translated coronavirus information together with the hotline information with interpreter information
