COVID-19 Response

The WSMA, Washington state officials, and the state's health care community have mobilized in response to an outbreak of the novel coronavirus SARS-CoV-2 and its associated respiratory illness, COVID-19.

The WSMA and nearly 30 medical societies across the state are urging Washington state's new COVID-19 response director, Vice Admiral Raquel C. Bono, MD, to: 1.) Globally assess the current supplies of PPE at the state level, and 2.) Create a more comprehensive distribution model that considers the critical role of ambulatory sites. Read the letter, delivered March 26, 2020.

The WSMA is urging our state's Congressional delegation officials to take action to secure financial relief for physician practices during the pandemic. Read the letter, delivered March 25, 2020, which details the measures urgently needed.

COVID-19 Response Updates in Brief

Brief updates on telehealth and reimbursement, clinical guidelines and testing, and caregiver mental health resources can be found on those respective pages.

The Washington Medical Commission has carried out emergency rulemaking to re-license retired physicians, waive CME requirements, and allow these people to receive payment for their efforts; as noted in today's Gov. press conference. The Federation of State Medical Boards has created a chart to show states that are following suit. (March 26, 2020)

The WSMA supports Gov. Inslee's action "stay home and stay healthy" order, mandating the closure of non-essential businesses and ordering Washington residents to stay at home and avoid any unnecessary travel. Read our press release. (March 23, 2020)

Gov. Inslee named retired Navy Vice Admiral Raquel C. Bono as head of the Washington state COVID-19 health care response team. Bono will advise the governor, his staff, and state agencies on actions needed to address the capacity and strain across the health care system. Read the press release. (March 22, 2020)

The WSMA joined five other medical associations in states impacted by COVID-19—California, Florida, Massachusetts, New York, and Texas—to send a letter to Congressional leaders urging urging inclusion of additional measures to protect practice viability and physician health into their COVID-19 response plans. Read the letter. (March 20, 2020)

Health practitioner volunteers needed: The Washington State Department of Health is enrolling and activating emergency volunteer health practitioners in preparation for health system requests and surging. This will help the state meet emerging demands for health care workers. Learn more. (March 20, 2020)

The US Small Business Administration is offering a low-interest loans to help small business owners meet financial obligations, cover operating expenses, and alleviate economic injury caused by COVID-19. Click here to determine your eligibility. For more information and to apply, click here. For questions, 800.659.2955 or disastercustomerservice@sba.gov. (March 20, 2020)

The WSMA and over 20 state specialty and county medical societies support Gov. Jay Inslee's proclamation prohibiting elective surgeries and procedures. Read WSMA's press release. (March 20, 2020)

The WSMA has expanded its COVID-19 response web resources. Now find dedicated pages for telehealth and reimbursement guidance, clinical and testing guidance, patient education resources, and caregiver mental health resources. (March 20, 2020)

The WSMA has introduced a new COVID-19 discussion forum for physicians and PAs. (March 20, 2020)

Our video PSAs and other patient education materials can now be found on their dedicated page. (March 20, 2020)

The WSMA has heard from state officials that each local health district is setting up plans for child care for health care workers. As those plans become available, we will share those details.

The WSMA delivered a letter, cosigned by more than 25 other state and specialty societies, to Washington Gov. Jay Inslee urging him to expand social distancing limitations to restrict gatherings of 50 or more. Additionally, the letter urged more flexibility on telehealth, so that physicians are able to continue caring for their chronically ill patients. (March 13, 2020)
