Legislative Summit

The WSMA will hold the 2021 WSMA Legislative Summit virtually on Tuesday, February 9, 2021 from 5:30-8:30 p.m.  as the Capitol is closed to the public in order to safely follow public health guidelines. The event is free for WSMA members, so now is a great time to join WSMA or renew your membership ! There will be a $30 registration fee for nonmember, non-physician/non-physician assistant attendees.

The WSMA has tentatively secured both Washington State Secretary of Health Umair Shah, MD, MPH , who will offer an update on the state’s COVID-19 response efforts, and Speaker of the House Laurie Jinkins , who will share her perspective on the legislative session currently underway, to join us at this year’s virtual event. If you would like to submit a question to either of these speakers, please email your question to alex@wsma.org in advance. We won’t be able to get to all questions that are submitted, but we will do our best to cover an array of key issues.

This year’s session will be dominated by issues of import to the physician community, as legislators seek to respond to the pandemic, balance the state’s budget and continue to reform the state’s health care system. During the Summit you will receive an update on everything happening in Olympia, including WSMA’s legislative priorities:

WSMA 2021 Legislative Priorities (subject to change)

  • Fiscal
    • Medicaid reimbursement increases
    • Foundational Public Health Services funding
    • Taxes
  • Policy
    • Ensuring coverage and reimbursement for audio-only telemedicine services
    • Requiring carriers to reimburse for extraordinary PPE costs
    • Promoting health equity through health professional continuing education
    • Installing appropriate pandemic-related liability protections
    • Preventing inappropriate scope of practice increases

Learn more about WSMA’s 2021 legislative priorities . While WSMA’s government affairs team continues to advocate for the house of medicine in a new virtual setting, there’s still no substitute for physicians connecting directly with their local legislators. Given the structure of this year’s session and Legislative Summit, WSMA will not be prearranging meetings with your legislative delegation this year, though we have created resources on how to connect with your legislator’s office to schedule a meeting that works with your schedule and one-pagers on WSMA’s priority issues in help inform your virtual advocacy.

We look forward to seeing you all at the 2021 Legislative Summit on February 9.
