New Physician Assistant Practice Laws and Regulations: Putting HB2378 into Practice

Tuesday, June 8 from noon–1 p.m.

A key priority for the WSMA and the Washington Academy of Physician Assistants, House Bill 2378 from 2020 made important changes to the state’s physician assistant practice act, modernizing the profession and facilitating employment of these valued clinical partners. Major provisions of this important legislation take effect July 1. Speakers will outline how to implement the changes in your practice and discuss how to create practice agreements, which are taking the place of the current delegation agreements.

Fundamentals of Diagnostic Error in Imaging

Tuesday, June 22 from noon–1 p.m.

Jason N. Itri, MD, PhD, President of Itri Consulting Group, LLC and Director of Quality and Associate Medical Director at Nines Radiology PA, will identify the components of a comprehensive process to learn from diagnostic error in radiology. He will describe the different approaches to identifying cases with diagnostic error and discuss educational and systematic interventions that can reduce diagnostic errors.

This webinar is part of a series co-sponsored by the Washington State Hospital Association and Washington State Medical Association on diagnostic excellence. The Northwest Safety and Quality Partnership along with the Medical Officer Collaborative have both underscored the priority of diagnostic excellence as a quality and patient safety measure. The effects of diagnostic errors are currently estimated to affect upwards of 12 million Americans each year, causing more harm to patients than all other hospital errors combined. These errors also have a marked impact on health care providers; the emotional effects of diagnostic error can be long-lasting and harmful. Patients identify the ramifications of diagnostic errors as emotional distress, prolonged illness and medical complications, impairment in activities of daily living, and decreased confidence in the health care system. Given the prevalence and impacts of diagnostic errors, health care leaders must address them as part of their quality improvement and patient safety programs.
